AI Maturity Model

From AI pilots to governed, enterprise-scale AI.

Truzen’s AI Maturity Model provides a structured view of how organizations move from isolated experiments to integrated, governed AI. It combines numeric levels (1–4) with clear descriptive characteristics at each stage.

Within Truzen’s advisory engagements, this model is used as a neutral, leadership-friendly diagnostic to frame where you are today, where governance gaps exist, and what it takes to scale AI responsibly.

Overview

A practical, four-level view of AI maturity

The model below is designed to be pragmatic and leadership-friendly. It is not tied to any single sector or technology stack. Instead, it focuses on observable behavior across strategy, data, governance, risk, and operating model dimensions, so that leadership, oversight, and delivery teams share a common language for AI readiness.

Level Label Short description
Level 1 Ad-hoc Experiments Individual teams run AI experiments and proofs of concept, with limited coordination and minimal formal governance.
Level 2 Structured Pilots Priority pilots are selected, tracked, and supported by initial strategy, data, and governance structures.
Level 3 Scaled & Governed AI Multiple AI solutions operate across the organization with defined governance, data practices, and lifecycle oversight.
Level 4 Integrated AI Enterprise AI is embedded into strategy, operating models, and governance in a repeatable, accountable way across functions and business units.

The model is intended as a directional guide, not a certification. It supports structured conversations about “where we are”, “how governance currently works”, and “what needs to change next”.

Levels

Level-by-level characteristics

Level 1

Ad-hoc Experiments

AI work is driven by interested teams or individuals rather than by a coordinated organizational view.

  • Multiple disconnected proofs of concept
  • Limited linkage to enterprise strategy
  • Data access arranged case by case
  • Governance and risk rarely involved early
Level 2

Structured Pilots

AI pilots are selected, prioritized, and monitored with clearer objectives and emerging governance patterns.

  • Visible list of priority AI pilots
  • Initial alignment with business objectives
  • Identified data sources and owners
  • Early involvement of governance and risk in higher-impact pilots
Level 3

Scaled & Governed AI

AI is used in multiple processes or products. Governance, data, and lifecycle practices are defined and repeatable.

  • Portfolio view of AI solutions in production
  • Defined lifecycle checkpoints and documentation
  • Data governance supports key AI use cases
  • Risk and oversight functions have structured roles
Level 4

Integrated AI Enterprise

AI is systematically integrated into strategy, operating models, governance, and risk structures.

  • AI is part of strategy and planning cycles
  • Data and AI governance are tightly linked
  • Operating model embeds AI roles and workflows
  • Leadership and oversight use consistent AI metrics and evidence

Dimensions

Maturity dimensions we typically assess

The numeric maturity level is informed by observable patterns across multiple dimensions. Each dimension can progress at a different pace; the model provides a consolidated view for leadership discussions and for designing targeted governance and risk interventions.

Strategy & portfolio

How AI initiatives are prioritized, funded, and tracked against mission or business objectives, including how AI value and risk are surfaced to leadership.

Data & foundations

Readiness of data ownership, quality, lineage, and access practices to support AI that must be explainable and accountable.

Governance & risk

Clarity of roles, decision pathways, and lifecycle checkpoints for AI solutions, including documentation, approvals, and evidence of control.

Operating model & roles

How responsibilities are distributed across business, data, technology, and oversight functions for AI work, and how they interact in practice.

Practices & lifecycle

The consistency of methods and documentation across the AI lifecycle: idea, design, build, validation, deployment, and monitoring, including how model changes are governed over time.

People & readiness

Awareness, skills, and comfort levels of leadership, practitioners, and oversight functions in using and supervising AI, including how they interpret AI-related evidence and risk.

From model to roadmap

How the maturity model connects to transformation

The maturity model is a diagnostic tool. It becomes more powerful when paired with a transformation framework that describes how to move from one level to the next. Truzen uses the Level 1–4 view as input into a structured transformation roadmap, focusing on governance, operating model, and risk capabilities—not only technology.

Level 1 → Level 2

Move from scattered experiments to visible, prioritized pilots with basic governance and data clarity in place.

Level 2 → Level 3

Evolve pilot practices into repeatable patterns with clear lifecycle checkpoints, data stewardship, and risk involvement.

Level 3 → Level 4

Integrate AI into enterprise strategy, operating models, and governance so that AI becomes part of “how we work”, not a side program.
