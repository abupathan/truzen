Context

Why AI in financial services requires disciplined governance

Financial institutions operate in environments where decisions—credit approvals, policy underwriting, claims settlement, transaction monitoring, risk scoring, product eligibility, and pricing adjustments—must be defendable and aligned with established risk practices. AI amplifies both the opportunity and the governance expectations around these decisions.

High-impact decisions

AI influences outcomes such as creditworthiness, underwriting decisions, fraud detection, and financial product recommendations—requiring transparency and consistent oversight across banking and insurance portfolios.

Model governance expectations

AI models in financial services must be explainable, documented, and reviewed through structured governance to ensure alignment with institutional risk appetite, model risk standards, and regulatory expectations.

Data lineage & trust

Data inputs for financial decisions must be traceable and high-quality to support accountability and audit needs across key decision flows in retail, commercial, and insurance businesses.

Segments we support

Banking and insurance, with shared and segment-specific needs

While banking and insurance share common expectations on governance and risk, the way AI is applied—credit decisioning vs. underwriting and claims—differs in important ways. Truzen’s work recognises both the shared foundations and segment-specific realities.

Insurance

Advisory for insurers applying AI in underwriting, pricing, claims triage, and customer interactions, where transparency and regulatory expectations are particularly pronounced.
