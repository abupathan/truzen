Context

Why AI governance matters in K12, higher education, and EdTech

AI in education touches learners, educators, institutional leadership, and product ecosystems. From adaptive learning to analytics to administrative automation, AI systems must be explainable, appropriate, and accountable. Data about learners and educators carries heightened expectations around stewardship and trust.

Impact on learner pathways

AI-driven recommendations and insights influence learning paths, support decisions, and interventions—requiring clarity on how decisions are made and how they can be challenged or explained.

Institutional accountability

Leaders must understand, oversee, and defend how AI systems inform academic, operational, and administrative choices across K12 and higher education settings.

Data stewardship expectations

Learner and educator data must be governed with strong sensitivity, lineage awareness, and clear boundaries on what is appropriate and permitted use.
