AI Strategy

From AI pilots to an enterprise-scale, governed AI roadmap.

Truzen’s AI Strategy advisory helps leadership clarify where AI should play, which initiatives matter most, and how to move from experiments to a sequenced, investable roadmap that is grounded in data readiness, governance, and risk expectations.

Leadership questions

AI strategy is about making a small number of clear, durable choices.

Most organizations already have AI activity in motion: pilots, proofs of concept, or early production systems. What is often missing is a clear view on where to focus next, how to prioritize, and what guardrails are needed so AI contributes to outcomes rather than creating noise or unmanaged risk.

Truzen’s AI Strategy work is designed around executive-level questions: where does AI create real value, what is realistically feasible given your data and operating context, and how should you stage investments over the next 12–36 months.

Typical questions we help answer

  • Where should we focus first, and what should wait?
  • Which use cases are truly viable given our data and constraints?
  • How do we balance experimentation with control and oversight?
  • What should our AI investment roadmap look like over time?
  • How do we ensure AI plans align with data, governance, and risk work?

How we structure AI strategy work

From ambition and portfolio to value, feasibility, and roadmap.

AI Strategy engagements are anchored in a small set of workstreams that keep the conversation grounded: ambition, portfolio, value, feasibility, governance alignment, and roadmap. Each workstream can be dialed up or down depending on where your organization is starting.

Ambition & guiding principles

Clarify why AI matters for your organization and what “good” looks like.

  • Define AI ambition aligned to business or mission outcomes.
  • Agree on time horizons and risk appetite for AI adoption.
  • Set guiding principles for responsible and accountable AI use.
Often co-led by executive sponsors

Use case portfolio & prioritization

Move from a long list of ideas to a portfolio that reflects value, feasibility, and governance considerations.

  • Inventory current and proposed AI use cases.
  • Score initiatives against value, feasibility, and risk profile.
  • Identify “no-regret” pilots and strategic bets.
Uses Truzen’s AI Maturity Model & portfolio lenses

Value & outcome definition

Make AI outcomes explicit so progress can be measured and governed.

  • Link AI initiatives to financial, operational, or mission metrics.
  • Define what “proof of value” looks like for each initiative.
  • Agree on simple measurement and reporting expectations.
Prevents “AI for AI’s sake”

Data & platform readiness

Align AI ambitions with what is realistic from a data and platform perspective.

  • Assess data availability, quality, lineage, and access.
  • Understand current analytics and AI platform capabilities.
  • Identify foundational gaps to address via Data Strategy work.
Linked to: Data Strategy & Data Governance

Governance, risk & guardrails

Ensure AI choices are consistent with governance and risk expectations.

  • Map where oversight is required across the AI lifecycle.
  • Identify risk-sensitive use cases needing stronger guardrails.
  • Align with emerging responsible AI and regulatory expectations.
Linked to: AI Governance & AI Risk Assurance

Roadmap & phasing

Translate ambition and portfolio into a realistic, staged plan.

  • Phase initiatives across near, mid, and longer-term horizons.
  • Align AI roadmap with data, governance, and operating model changes.
  • Define key decision points and review moments for leadership.
Feeds into: AI Operating Model & Programs

Engagement formats

Flexible formats for where you are on the AI journey.

AI Strategy work can be delivered as a focused sprint, a structured portfolio exercise, or a deeper strategy and governance design engagement. The formats below reflect what organizations most commonly request.

AI strategy sprint

A time-bound engagement to clarify ambition, focus areas, and immediate priorities without committing to a long program.

  • Workshop series with core leadership and functional heads.
  • Initial portfolio scan and priority use case short-list.
  • High-level roadmap for the next 6–12 months.
Good for: early-stage or re-baselining AI efforts

AI portfolio & roadmap design

A deeper working phase to structure the AI portfolio and build an investable, phased roadmap aligned to data and governance dependencies.

  • Detailed use case assessment across value, feasibility, and risk.
  • Definition of “no-regret” actions vs. strategic bets.
  • Integrated roadmap including data, platform, and governance enablers.
Good for: organizations with active AI pilots

AI strategy & governance blueprint

A comprehensive engagement that connects AI strategy with data, governance, risk, and operating model work.

  • End-to-end AI strategy articulation and portfolio design.
  • Alignment with Data Strategy, AI Governance, and AI Risk frameworks.
  • Input into operating model, committees, and oversight mechanisms.
Good for: organizations scaling AI across functions
